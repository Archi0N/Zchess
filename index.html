A fully legal chess duel on an 8x8 board. Face another player or train against a static Black AI without leaving the page.

Rules

  • White moves first. In Play vs AI, you control White and choose Black AI difficulty: Beginner, Medium, Hard, or Master.
  • Click one of your pieces, then a highlighted destination.
  • Pieces use standard chess movement: pawns move forward, capture diagonally, may advance two squares on their first move, and auto-promote to a Queen on the back rank.
  • Rooks, bishops, and queens slide. Knights jump. Kings move one square, and castling is allowed only when the King and rook are unmoved, the path is clear, and the King does not move through check.
  • En passant is supported immediately after an adjacent pawn makes a two-square advance.
  • You cannot make a move that leaves your own King in check. Kings are not captured as legal moves.
  • Checks are announced in the status bar. Checkmate ends the game with GAME OVER and the winner text: White win or Black win.
  • If no legal move exists without check, the game is a stalemate draw.
  • Use Pause to freeze the board, Continue to resume, Save/Load to store progress, and Undo Move to go back. In AI mode, Undo removes the AI reply and your previous move together.

Piece Guide

♔

King

Moves 1 square in any direction and may castle if neither the King nor rook has moved and the route is safe.

♕

Queen

Slides any number of squares horizontally, vertically, or diagonally.

♖

Rook

Slides any number of squares horizontally or vertically.

♗

Bishop

Slides any number of squares diagonally.

♘

Knight

Jumps in an L shape: 2 in one direction, then 1 perpendicular.

♙

Pawn

Moves forward 1, may advance 2 from its starting row, captures diagonally, promotes to a Queen, and can use en passant.
